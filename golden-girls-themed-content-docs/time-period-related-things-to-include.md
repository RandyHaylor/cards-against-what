# Time Period Context

The Golden Girls aired 1985-1992 on NBC and was set in contemporary Miami during those years.

Cards and references should feel grounded in the mid-1980s to early 1990s:

## Technology
- No internet, no cell phones, no email
- Landline phones, answering machines
- VCRs, cable TV, TV Guide
- Polaroid cameras

## Culture
- Personal ads in the newspaper (no dating apps)
- Shopping at department stores and malls
- Soap operas as daytime entertainment
- Game shows (Wheel of Fortune, Jeopardy)
- Aerobics and Jane Fonda workout tapes

## Miami / Florida
- Collins Avenue, South Beach before the Art Deco revival
- Early-bird dinner specials
- Retirement communities, condo associations
- Hurricane season
- Publix grocery stores

## Music / Entertainment
- Big band nostalgia, Frank Sinatra, Tony Bennett
- Lawrence Welk
- Johnny Carson / Tonight Show
- Dynasty, Dallas, Murder She Wrote

## Social Norms
- Senior dating through church socials, community events, personal ads
- Prescriptions picked up in person at the pharmacy
- Letters and phone calls, not texts
- "Long distance" phone charges were a real thing
- The phrase "gentleman caller"

## Adult Themes on Network TV
The show was groundbreaking for addressing taboo subjects openly in a primetime sitcom:
- Menopause
- Sex and STDs
- LGBTQ+ issues
- Death and illness
- Financial insecurity

## Women's Rights & Feminism
Recurring themes:
- Equal pay
- Workplace discrimination
- Ageism
- Reproductive rights (discussed in past-tense contexts)

The characters were written as financially independent and socially autonomous — reflecting second-wave feminist normalization.

## Healthcare & Elder Policy
- Nursing home conditions
- Medicare
- Elder abuse
- End-of-life decisions

## Money / Economy
- A nice dinner out might cost $25-40
- Rent and mortgage were real concerns on fixed incomes
- Coupons, early-bird specials, senior discounts were survival strategies
