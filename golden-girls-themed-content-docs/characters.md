# Golden Girls — Character Reference Guide
=============================================

## MAIN CHARACTERS

### Dorothy Zbornak (née Petrillo, later Hollingsworth)
**Played by:** Bea Arthur
**Background:** Italian-American substitute teacher from Brooklyn, NY. Daughter of Sicilian immigrants. Got pregnant in high school, married Stan Zbornak for 38 years until he left her for a flight attendant. Two children: Kate and Michael. Marries Blanche's uncle Lucas Hollingsworth in the series finale.
**Age:** ~55 at series start
**Personality:** Sarcastic, intelligent, dry-witted, the voice of reason. Short-tempered but deeply loyal. Avid reader. The "straight man" of the group. Often self-deprecating about her love life (or lack thereof).
**Catchphrases / Signature Lines:**
- "Shady Pines, Ma!" (threatening to send Sophia back to the nursing home)
- Withering sarcasm aimed at Rose's stories and Blanche's love life
- Deep eye-rolls and deadpan stares
**Style/Clothing:** Tailored, pulled-together. Broad-shouldered blazers, long vests, structured outfits. Tall and imposing — the show frequently made jokes about her height and frame.
**Quirks:** Loves diagramming sentences. Follows current events. Can't resist correcting people. Chronic insomnia that leads to late-night kitchen talks. Still has complicated feelings about Stan despite the divorce.
**Comedic Role:** The reactor — her exasperated expressions and cutting one-liners puncture everyone else's absurdity.

### Sophia Petrillo
**Played by:** Estelle Getty (wore aging makeup — was actually younger than Bea Arthur)
**Background:** Born and raised in Sicily. Immigrated to America. Married Salvadore "Sal" Petrillo. Suffered a stroke that destroyed the part of her brain that filters what she says (the show's canonical explanation for her bluntness). Lived at Shady Pines nursing home until it burned down; moved in with Dorothy.
**Age:** ~80 at series start
**Personality:** Brutally honest, sharp-tongued, sly, streetwise. Tells long stories that start with "Picture it..." Has old-world wisdom mixed with zero filter. Deeply loving underneath the insults. Competitive. Scheming when she wants something.
**Catchphrases / Signature Lines:**
- "Picture it: Sicily, 19__..."
- "Pussycat" (pet name for Dorothy)
- "You're bluffing..." (responding to Dorothy's Shady Pines threat)
- Endless insults about Dorothy's looks, love life, and age
- Slut-shaming Blanche at every opportunity
- Calling Rose stupid in creative ways
**Style/Clothing:** Comfortable, practical. Wicker handbag she carries everywhere. Housecoats, cardigans, low-heeled shoes. Short stature — 4'11". The purse is iconic; it contained everything she could possibly need.
**Quirks:** Carries her wicker purse at all times. Had a stroke that removed her verbal filter. Plays cards. Makes Sicilian dishes. Has an encyclopedic memory for neighborhood gossip from decades ago. Fiercely protective of Dorothy despite constantly insulting her. Competitive with her sister Angela.
**Comedic Role:** The insult machine — delivers the most savage one-liners with grandmotherly innocence.

---

### Blanche Devereaux
**Played by:** Rue McClanahan
**Background:** Southern belle from Atlanta, Georgia. Wealthy upbringing by her father "Big Daddy." Widow of George Devereaux. Owns the house where all four women live. Has several children including Rebecca and Clayton (who comes out as gay). Works at a museum.
**Personality:** Flirtatious, vain, confident, man-crazy. Deeply insecure about aging. Self-described Southern magnolia. Sees herself as irresistible. Actually kind and loyal beneath the vanity. Generous — she did open her home to the others.
**Catchphrases / Signature Lines:**
- "I treat my body like a temple."
- References to being a "devastatingly beautiful woman"
- Constant name-dropping of suitors, beaus, and gentleman callers
- Dramatic Southern declarations
- "When I say jump, you say 'on who?'"
**Style/Clothing:** Sexy, soft, and flowing. Low-cut tops, silk robes, form-fitting dresses. Flashy jewelry. Always impeccably made up, even at home. Nightgowns were always glamorous. The most fashion-forward of the group.
**Quirks:** Lies about her age constantly. Her bed has "more miles on it than the Spirit of St. Louis." Obsessed with her appearance. Competitive with other women for male attention. Has a younger-man weakness. Takes extremely long baths. Can't resist flirting, even at funerals. Her Southern accent gets thicker when she's emotional.
**Comedic Role:** The provocateur — her sexual confidence and vanity set up jokes that everyone else can knock down.

---

### Rose Nylund (née Lindström)
**Played by:** Betty White
**Background:** Norwegian-American from St. Olaf, Minnesota — an absurdly quirky small farming town. Widow of Charlie Nylund, who died while they were making love (giving her intimacy issues). Works at a grief counseling center. Later dates Miles Webber. Was adopted — discovered her biological father later in the series.
**Personality:** Sweet, naïve, trusting, kind to a fault. Not stupid, but sheltered — her frame of reference is entirely St. Olaf, which is a deeply weird place. Genuinely good-hearted. Occasionally reveals surprising depth or delivers an accidental burn.
**Catchphrases / Signature Lines:**
- "Back in St. Olaf..." (beginning of every tangential story)
- "As they say in St. Olaf: [gibberish Scandinavian-sounding phrase]"
- Bizarre St. Olaf proverbs involving herring, livestock, and inexplicable customs
- "Charlie used to say..."
**Style/Clothing:** Down-home, wholesome. Soft colors, floral prints, sweaters, modest necklines. The most conservative dresser. Looked like a Minnesota farm wife transplanted to Miami. Comfortable and approachable.
**Quirks:** Every conversation reminds her of a St. Olaf story. The stories are always insanely convoluted, involve characters with unpronounceable Scandinavian names, and never seem to have a point. Competitive about being "the nice one." Has a surprisingly wild side that surfaces rarely. Her late husband Charlie is referenced constantly. Her cousin Sven did something bizarre in every other story.
**Comedic Role:** The innocent — her wide-eyed cluelessness creates setup after setup for everyone else's punchlines.

## MAJOR RECURRING CHARACTERS

### Stanley "Stan" Zbornak
**Played by:** Herb Edelman (appeared in 26 episodes — most of any recurring character)
**Background:** Dorothy's ex-husband. Left her after 38 years for a young flight attendant named Chrissy. Unsuccessful novelty item salesman who eventually invented the "Zbornie" (a baked potato opener) and struck it rich. Always scheming, always showing up uninvited.
**Signature:** "Hi, it's me, Stan." (always announces himself this way)
**Quirks:** Wears a terrible, unconvincing toupee. Constant get-rich-quick schemes. Still pines for Dorothy despite everything. Sophia despises him. Everyone in the house groans when he appears. Has a pathetic puppy-dog quality that occasionally makes him sympathetic.
**Comedic Role:** The punching bag — exists to be insulted by Sophia, rejected by Dorothy, and exasperated at by everyone.

### Miles Webber (real name: Nicholas Carbone)
**Played by:** Harold Gould (14 episodes)
**Background:** Rose's boyfriend from season 5 onward. College professor. Secret: he's actually a former mafia accountant in the witness protection program. Kind, intellectual, patient. Made Rose feel insecure about her intelligence initially but came to accept and love her.
**Quirks:** Bookish, proper, occasionally fussy. The reveal of his mob past was a major storyline. Genuinely sweet with Rose.

### Big Daddy Hollingsworth
**Played by:** Murray Hamilton / David Wayne
**Background:** Blanche's father from Atlanta. Larger-than-life Southern patriarch. Adores Blanche. His visits were big events.

### Clayton Hollingsworth
**Played by:** Monte Markham
**Background:** Blanche's brother who comes out as gay in a groundbreaking storyline. Later introduces his fiancé. Blanche struggles to accept but ultimately does.

### Gloria Petrillo
**Background:** Dorothy's younger sister. Was wealthy in California, later lost her money. Dated Stan in a late-season storyline that infuriated Dorothy.

### Michael Zbornak
**Played by:** Scott Jacoby
**Background:** Dorothy and Stan's son. Aimless musician. Sporadic appearances. A bit of a mama's boy.

### Charlie Nylund (deceased)
**Background:** Rose's late husband. Never appeared on screen but was referenced constantly. Died during lovemaking. Rose idealized him — he was the love of her life. His death was both tragic and the source of many jokes.

### George Devereaux (deceased)
**Background:** Blanche's late husband. Appeared once in a dream sequence. Blanche worshipped his memory. Their relationship was portrayed as genuinely loving.

### Salvadore "Sal" Petrillo (deceased)
**Background:** Sophia's late husband. Known through flashbacks and Sophia's stories. His reported last words: "Ten bucks says I don't need this oxygen tank."

## RECURRING LOCATIONS / CULTURAL TOUCHSTONES

- **The house in Miami** — Blanche's home where all four women live
- **The kitchen** — where late-night cheesecake conversations happen (the show's emotional core)
- **The lanai** — outdoor patio where they gather
- **Shady Pines** — the nursing home Sophia escaped from; Dorothy's go-to threat
- **St. Olaf, Minnesota** — Rose's hometown, source of all bizarre stories
- **Sicily** — Sophia's homeland, source of all "Picture it" stories
- **The Rusty Anchor** — a bar they sometimes visit
- **Sophia's wicker purse** — practically a character itself
- **Cheesecake** — the ritual comfort food; appears in nearly every emotional scene
